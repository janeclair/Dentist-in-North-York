Knocked-Out Tooth?

Time is critical. Follow these steps immediately:

  1. 1. Pick up the tooth by the crown, not the root.
  2. 2. Rinse gently with milk if dirty — don't scrub.
  3. 3. Keep it moist in milk or your saliva.
  4. 4. Arrive within 60 minutes for best results.
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I see a dentist in North York? +

Every six months for routine care. Patients with gum disease, diabetes, or high cavity risk may need visits every three to four months.

Do you accept walk-in patients? +

Yes, we welcome walk-ins Monday through Saturday. Wait times are usually under 30 minutes. Calling ahead lets us prioritize your case.

Are dental X-rays safe? +

Yes. Digital X-rays use up to 90% less radiation than traditional film. We take them only when clinically necessary.

How long do dental implants last? +

With proper care, implants routinely last 25+ years — often a lifetime. The crown on top may need replacement after 15–20 years due to normal wear.

What if I have dental anxiety? +

Tell us. We offer nitrous oxide, oral sedation, noise-canceling headphones, and a patient-controlled stop signal. Many anxious patients wish they'd come sooner.

Do you accept dental insurance? +

Yes. We work with Sun Life, Manulife, Canada Life, Green Shield, Desjardins, and most major Canadian providers. We submit claims directly on your behalf.
